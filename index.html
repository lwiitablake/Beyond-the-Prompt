Why this approach scales

Repeatable

What should you do? Use the same decision steps each time.

Why is this better for learners? A repeatable process reduces rushed choices and makes support more consistent.

Tool-independent

What should you do? Focus on the decision, not the AI tool.

Why is this better for learners? Good teaching decisions still matter when tools change.

Learner-centered

What should you do? Ask whether the result better supports learners.

Why is this better for learners? The goal is not faster content. The goal is clearer, safer, more usable learning.

Information Cards

Information Cards explain key ideas. They teach; they do not make the decision for you.

Human Judgment

What should you know? AI output still needs expert review.

Why does it matter? Learners deserve accurate, accessible, and context-aware materials.

Accessibility

What should you know? Accessibility means content can be used by people with different disabilities and access needs.

Why does it matter? Accessible design reduces barriers before learners have to ask for help.

Plain Language

What should you know? Plain language means clear, direct wording your audience can understand the first time.

Why does it matter? Clear wording lowers cognitive load and helps busy learners act quickly.

Decision Cards

Decision Cards help you choose an action. Each card ends with a next step.

Decision Card

Should AI be used here?

Ask: Does this involve private learner information?

If yes: Do not use a public AI tool.

Ask: Does this affect grades, eligibility, discipline, or accommodations?

If yes: Keep human judgment central and check policy.

Next step: Use AI only when the task is low-risk, reviewable, and policy-safe.

Decision Card

Is this ready for learners?

Check: Can learners tell what to do first?

Check: Are directions, links, files, images, and media accessible?

Check: Does this support the learning goal?

Next step: Publish only after clarity, accessibility, and alignment are checked.

Decision Card

Can I upload this?

Ask: Does it include names, grades, IDs, messages, accommodations, or submissions?

If yes: Do not upload it to a public AI tool.

Ask: Does it include copyrighted or licensed material?

If yes: Check the license first.

Next step: Remove private information or use an approved institutional tool.
